## Business objective

Build a sustainable global platform whose commercial incentives reinforce athlete ownership, machine independence, trustworthy competition, and community growth. Revenue must come from delivering services—not from quietly monetizing personal data or creating artificial lock-in.

## Value network

### Athletes

Receive a portable workout history, progress, Athlete Passport, challenges, River Expeditions, event discovery, rankings, and privacy controls.

### Machine providers

Gain access to a broader athlete ecosystem, provider attribution, integration certification, compatible event participation, and reduced pressure to build every community feature independently.

### Clubs and coaches

Gain permissioned group management, challenges, summaries, rosters, and athlete collaboration.

### Organizers and federations

Gain event publishing, discovery, entries or registration handoffs, structured result intake, verification, communications, and reporting.

### Sponsors and mission partners

Gain clearly labeled participation programs, River Expeditions, or event sponsorship without acquiring athlete data by default.

## Revenue principles

- Core athlete history and data portability should remain accessible.
- Paying must not make an unverified result appear more official.
- Rankings and event eligibility cannot be sold as favoritism.
- Providers do not buy exclusive control over canonical categories.
- Sponsors receive aggregated, privacy-safe outcomes—not athlete contact lists.
- Official-partner revenue cannot make the product unusable without that partner.
- Any commercial use of athlete data requires a clear separate product and lawful consent; the default model does not depend on it.

## Candidate revenue streams

### Athlete subscription

A free tier supports account, core history, basic progress, Passport, privacy, export, and participation. A paid athlete tier may add advanced analytics, deeper comparisons, richer exports, training planning, expanded media storage, custom seasons, family features, or premium expedition experiences.

Do not charge athletes to retrieve their own data, delete an account, access essential consent controls, or see basic source provenance.

### Club and coach plans

Subscription based on active coaches, athletes, or teams for permissioned dashboards, programs, group analytics, challenge administration, roster tools, and branded but platform-governed communications.

### Event services

Organizer fees for event publishing packages, registration support, result intake, verification operations, timing integrations, certificates, official result hosting, messaging, and reports. Local volunteer events need an affordable or free entry level to grow supply.

### Federation platform services

Contracts for verified organization channels, national event discovery, participation pathways, federation Passport credentials, official series, reporting, and integrations. Agreements must define data roles and cannot transfer broad athlete ownership.

### Provider integration and certification

Providers may pay for premium certification, integration support, sandbox access, conformance testing, event enablement, or joint programs. Basic inclusion decisions remain governed by technical quality and athlete demand, not solely payment.

### Sponsored challenges and River Expeditions

Brands, charities, destinations, and institutions may sponsor missions with clear labeling, appropriate content review, and privacy-safe reporting. Participation cannot silently opt athletes into marketing.

### Enterprise and gym programs

Commercial gyms, schools, universities, wellness programs, and training centers may pay for fleet connection, QR-based shared-machine flows, private challenges, aggregate usage, and member onboarding. Individual athlete records remain portable.

## Packaging hypotheses

### Free athlete

Core record, manual/OCR capture, selected integrations, basic statistics, event discovery, challenges, Passport, privacy controls, export, and deletion.

### Athlete Plus

Advanced trends, custom comparisons, enhanced records, planning, expanded source history, premium Expeditions, and richer sharing.

### Coach/Club

Permissioned athlete groups, team challenges, training review, rosters, role management, and organization pages.

### Organizer

Event administration, entries/results, verification, communications, official boards, exports, and support.

### Federation/Provider

Contracted platform capabilities, integrations, governance, service levels, and co-developed programs.

Packaging must be validated through interviews and willingness-to-pay tests before implementation.

## Go-to-market sequence

1. Recruit independent and multi-machine athletes around the universal record.
2. Prove retention through history, personal records, and challenges.
3. Add provider integrations that unlock concentrated athlete groups.
4. Seed event discovery with organizers and federations.
5. Launch a signature River Expedition to create global participation.
6. Convert coaches, clubs, and organizers through workflow value.
7. Pursue official federation and World Rowing partnerships from demonstrated athlete trust and operational capability.

## Marketplace and network effects

More providers increase athlete history coverage. More athletes make event distribution valuable. More events increase Passport and notification value. More verified results improve benchmark credibility. More clubs and Expeditions increase retention.

The network must avoid a winner-takes-all trap that harms athletes. Data portability, open adapter contracts, visible rule ownership, and non-exclusive participation are strategic safeguards.

## Unit economics to understand

- Acquisition cost by athlete, provider, club, and organizer channel.
- Activation and retention by capture method.
- OCR/storage/notification cost per active athlete.
- Integration build and maintenance cost per provider.
- Organizer support cost per event and result.
- Verification review cost per tier.
- Free-to-paid conversion and gross margin by plan.
- River Expedition content and moderation cost.

## Business risks

- Provider API restrictions or exclusivity.
- High integration-support burden.
- Low athlete willingness to pay for history alone.
- Competition integrity costs.
- Federation procurement cycles.
- Privacy or safeguarding incidents.
- Overreliance on one official partner.
- Community moderation and notification abuse.

Mitigation comes from phased scope, multiple revenue streams, strong governance, portable architecture, and measuring athlete value before expansion.
