## North star

Indoor Rowing Platform will become the digital home of indoor rowing worldwide: the place where any athlete can keep a lifelong training record, understand progress, find events and communities, prove achievements, and participate in the sport regardless of machine, location, affiliation, or level.

## The world we want to create

Today, an indoor rower's history is often scattered across machine dashboards, manufacturer apps, screenshots, spreadsheets, gym consoles, race systems, and memory. Changing equipment can break continuity. Training at home can feel disconnected from the sport. Event information is fragmented. Athlete identity is repeatedly recreated for different challenges and competitions.

In the future we are building, an athlete has one trusted Athlete Passport. A session on an RP3 at a club, a WaterRower at home, a Matrix machine in a hotel, a Technogym rower in a commercial gym, a Concept2 at a competition, or a future Bluetooth device can all enter one history with honest provenance. The athlete sees what is comparable, what is verified, and how every session contributes to personal goals and community missions.

Events become discoverable rather than accidental. Clubs remain valuable but optional. National federations and a potential World Rowing partner can reach interested athletes through verified, consented channels without taking ownership of athlete data. River Expeditions give the worldwide community ambitious shared journeys that remain meaningful to beginners and high performers alike.

## Why now

Indoor rowing is practiced across homes, commercial gyms, rowing clubs, schools, rehabilitation settings, training centers, and competitions. Connected machines are increasing, but connectivity is fragmented. The sport has no neutral athlete layer that spans providers and participation contexts.

At the same time, athletes expect portable identity, clear privacy controls, modern mobile experiences, trustworthy data, and relevant notifications. Organizers need better discovery and result workflows. Federations need consented digital reach. A neutral platform can align these needs without requiring one provider to become the operating system for the whole sport.

## Strategic position

We are not building a Concept2 clone, a generic fitness tracker, a social network with rowing content, or a machine marketplace. We are building sport infrastructure with an athlete product at its center.

Our defensible position comes from five connected assets:

1. A canonical, provider-neutral workout history.
2. A lifelong Athlete Passport with verifiable claims.
3. Fair, explicit competition and ranking rules across machine classes.
4. A global community graph built around rowing activity and missions.
5. A consent and communication layer trusted by athletes and official organizations.

## Ten-year ambition

If the platform succeeds:

- Indoor rowers expect every machine to connect to their Passport.
- Athletes carry records and achievements across equipment and organizations.
- Major virtual and physical events publish structured information and accept trusted results through the platform.
- Clubs and federations use the platform to grow participation without excluding unaffiliated athletes.
- River Expeditions become recognized global participation traditions.
- Rankings are trusted because comparability and verification are explicit.
- Athlete communication is relevant because consent, sender identity, and preferences are respected.
- World Rowing can partner with the platform without the product becoming dependent on a single institution.

## What success is not

Success is not the largest volume of personal data, the most notifications sent, the longest engagement feed, or a universal leaderboard that hides machine differences. It is not winning exclusivity from one provider at the cost of athlete choice.

Success means athletes repeatedly trust the platform as their primary indoor-rowing record and participation home. It means providers gain value by connecting, organizers reach qualified participants, federations grow the sport, and every participant understands that the athlete relationship remains athlete-controlled.

## Product horizons

### Horizon 1: Trusted record

Deliver excellent manual and photo-assisted logging, provider-ready architecture, history, personal records, source transparency, account controls, and useful mock-to-real product validation.

### Horizon 2: Athlete network

Introduce the Athlete Passport, profile permissions, event discovery, consented notifications, challenges, rankings by explicit classes, clubs, teams, and the first River Expeditions.

### Horizon 3: Competition infrastructure

Support organizer tooling, result verification, event operations, official communication channels, federation integrations, qualification logic, and international programs.

### Horizon 4: Global sport layer

Become the neutral identity and data fabric for indoor rowing, widely integrated across providers, organizers, clubs, gyms, and national federations. The platform remains World Rowing compatible, never dependent.
