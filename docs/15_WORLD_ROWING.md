## Position

Indoor Rowing Platform should be capable of supporting a future official World Rowing partnership while remaining independently useful, trusted, and financially viable. The governing posture is **World Rowing compatible, never dependent**. Compatibility means the product can meet appropriate global sport, event, identity, communication, governance, accessibility, and operational requirements. It does not mean claiming a current relationship.

## Non-negotiable representation rule

Until formal written authorization exists:

- Do not use World Rowing marks, official visual identity, or protected event branding.
- Do not label platform rankings, records, events, messages, or credentials as official World Rowing products.
- Do not imply approval, endorsement, data sharing, or governance.
- Attribute public information to its actual source.
- Describe the platform as "compatible with a future official partnership" or "partnership-ready," not affiliated.

The same standard applies to national federations and event organizers.

## Why compatibility matters

World Rowing compatibility creates discipline even before a partnership. A global governing-body use case requires:

- International athlete identity without manufacturer lock-in.
- Federation and club relationships.
- Global event and category models.
- Machine eligibility defined per competition.
- Trusted result verification and appeals.
- Consented communications from official senders.
- Safeguarding and minor protections.
- Adaptive and inclusive competition support.
- Localization, time zones, and cross-border data governance.
- Official sender and brand controls.
- Event-grade reliability and auditability.

These are valuable platform qualities regardless of partner outcome.

## Potential partnership use cases

### Global event discovery

Publish authoritative information for World Rowing indoor events, Virtual Series, Virtual World Championships, qualification pathways, and relevant national events. Athletes receive localized dates, eligibility, accepted machines, registration links, rules, and updates.

### Athlete Passport credentials

Issue or recognize scoped credentials for official participation, results, age/category eligibility, federation affiliation, qualifications, certificates, and records. Athletes approve presentation; the platform does not expose the full Passport.

### Official communications

Deliver verified messages to athletes who consented to relevant World Rowing categories. Use cases include event announcements, registration deadlines, schedule changes, qualification, safety, results, development, education, and research. Sender identity and reason for selection are explicit.

### Virtual competitions

Support rule publication, machine eligibility, entries, device/provider evidence, organizer review, rankings, result boards, certificates, appeals, and audit.

### Participation growth

Connect home, commercial-gym, school, club, and independent athletes to pathways in the wider sport. Aggregated reporting can show participation without transferring raw athlete profiles.

### River Expeditions and campaigns

Co-create global participation missions, heritage journeys, development campaigns, or charitable initiatives with approved branding, governance, and consent.

## Partnership principles

1. Athlete consent remains under platform control and applicable law.
2. World Rowing receives only data required for agreed purposes.
3. Official authority is scoped to named programs and roles.
4. Provider neutrality remains intact unless a competition explicitly defines eligible equipment.
5. The platform retains an independent brand and direct athlete value.
6. Athletes without clubs or federation membership remain first-class users.
7. Public statements distinguish official, sanctioned, listed, and community events.
8. Partnership termination cannot erase athlete-owned history or disable the core product.

## Data governance questions

Before integration, determine:

- Which party is controller, processor, joint controller, or independent controller for each purpose?
- What is the lawful basis for account, event, result, communication, safeguarding, and analytics data?
- What data is transferred, to whom, where, and for how long?
- How are athlete access, correction, export, deletion, objection, and consent withdrawal handled?
- Which results must be retained for sporting integrity?
- How are minors, guardians, and sensitive eligibility data handled?
- How are cross-border transfers governed?
- Who manages incidents, complaints, appeals, and regulator contact?
- What happens when the agreement ends?

## Brand governance

Official co-branding requires approved assets, placement rules, event scope, locale, review process, accessibility, sponsorship conflicts, correction process, and termination behavior. Official status should be data-driven through verified organization roles rather than hardcoded UI.

The independent platform identity remains visible so athletes understand which organization operates the account and which organization owns an event or message.

## Competition governance

World Rowing-owned rules must be versioned and attributed. Platform teams implement but do not reinterpret them without authority. Category definitions, sanctions, qualification, accepted machine classes, verification, records, protests, and appeals require named owners.

Automated eligibility or anomaly systems can assist officials but cannot silently make high-impact decisions without explainability and human review.

## Federation hierarchy

The model should support World Rowing, national federations, regional bodies, clubs, organizers, and delegated event staff through scoped, dated roles. A federation relationship does not make every club or event official. Verification and delegation need expiry and revocation.

## Technical readiness

- Organization and role-based access control.
- Strong authentication for officials.
- Immutable audit for results, rules, and communications.
- Versioned APIs and bulk data exchange.
- Localization and global time-zone handling.
- High-availability event operations and incident response.
- Evidence protection and retention controls.
- Consent enforcement at audience and delivery time.
- Data export, deletion, and legal-hold behavior.
- Provider/machine comparability rules per competition.

## Commercial readiness

A partnership business case should define service scope, service levels, implementation, support, event volume, certification, brand value, sponsorship conflicts, data costs, liability, insurance, and termination. Avoid exclusivity that prevents supporting other legitimate providers or organizers unless the athlete benefit is exceptional, time-bound, and explicitly approved.

## Partnership stages

1. Public-data compatibility and informal discovery.
2. Non-branded technical pilot with one event or communication use case.
3. National federation pilots and operational evidence.
4. Formal World Rowing limited-scope partnership.
5. Expanded official programs based on measured athlete and organizer value.

Each stage requires a go/no-go decision in [99_PRODUCT_DECISIONS.md](99_PRODUCT_DECISIONS.md).

## Independence test

Before accepting a partnership term, ask:

- Does the platform still serve athletes if the agreement ends tomorrow?
- Can athletes retain and export their history?
- Can other machine providers remain integrated?
- Is official authority clearly scoped?
- Are communication choices still meaningful?
- Are unaffiliated athletes still welcome?
- Would we defend this term publicly to athletes?

If the answer to any is no, the term requires redesign or rejection.
