## Community thesis

Indoor rowing community should be built around doing, progressing, helping, and participating. The platform does not need an infinite general-purpose feed to create belonging. Shared workouts, encouragement, challenges, teams, clubs, events, recognition, and long-term missions provide stronger context and healthier incentives.

## Inclusion model

Community must work for:

- Athletes who train alone at home.
- Athletes using shared commercial-gym machines.
- Club and school athletes.
- Virtual teams.
- Beginners and returning athletes.
- Performance and competition athletes.
- Adaptive athletes.
- Athletes without a compatible connected machine.

Club membership is optional. Independent athletes can join public challenges, River Expeditions, rankings where eligible, and virtual crews.

## Relationship types

### Training partners

Mutual relationship for sharing selected workouts, progress, and encouragement. Visibility is explicit and revocable.

### Following

One-way subscription to public achievements or organizer updates. Following does not reveal private workouts or contact data.

### Coach-athlete

Explicit, scoped, time-bound access to selected training information. The athlete can revoke access; the coach does not own the account.

### Club membership

Dated organizational relationship with roles such as athlete, coach, captain, and administrator. Historical results retain the affiliation at the time.

### Teams and crews

Temporary or recurring groups for a challenge, event, or River Expedition. Team membership does not automatically change club affiliation.

## River Expeditions

### Concept

River Expeditions are long-term community missions in which individual or crew rowing distance advances along a mapped river journey. They turn cumulative training into shared exploration and storytelling while remaining machine-independent.

Examples could include stages inspired by the Nile, Amazon, Danube, Mississippi, Mekong, Rhine, or other culturally and ecologically significant waterways. Names, maps, stories, and partner content require geographic, cultural, and rights review.

### Product goals

- Motivate long-term consistency beyond short streaks.
- Give non-competitive athletes meaningful participation.
- Connect global athletes through a common mission.
- Create milestones, learning, charity, and partner opportunities.
- Make every eligible meter matter without claiming machines are performance-equivalent.

### Rules

Expeditions define dates, eligible activity, machine/source eligibility, distance conversion where needed, individual/crew sizes, milestone logic, evidence requirements, privacy, and contribution caps if required. Participation-only distance can accept broader sources than competitive rankings because the objective is progress, not direct performance comparison.

### Experience

Athletes see route progress, personal contribution, crew contribution, upcoming milestones, stories, environmental or cultural context, and completion artifacts. Notifications are consented and paced. The experience should not expose precise athlete locations.

### Integrity

Contribution comes from canonical workouts. Suspicious volume can be flagged; verification tiers can be displayed or required for special leaderboards. The core expedition should remain inclusive even when an optional competitive board uses stricter rules.

## Challenges

Challenges can be personal, public, club, team, provider, organizer, federation, charitable, or official partner programs. Types include distance, time, days active, consistency, standard effort, improvement, relay, and contribution.

Automatic enrollment is allowed only when the athlete explicitly joined or when the challenge is purely private and clearly dismissible. Public boards require separate visibility choices.

## Recognition

Recognition includes:

- Personal bests.
- Season bests.
- Challenge completion.
- Expedition milestones.
- Consistency and return milestones.
- Club/team contribution.
- Verified competition results.
- Long-term distance achievements.
- Community contribution or volunteering where verified.

Avoid a culture where only speed or volume counts. Never design rewards that encourage unsafe training or disadvantage athletes with less equipment access.

## Interaction model

Early community interactions should be constrained and positive: predefined encouragement, reactions, comments on explicitly shared achievements, team messages, and organizer announcements. Private messaging should wait until moderation, blocking, reporting, age controls, and abuse response are mature.

## Moderation and safety

Required capabilities include block, mute, report, content visibility, organizer verification, rate limits, anti-spam controls, audit logs, escalation paths, and age-appropriate communication. Public names and demographics must follow Passport visibility. Federation or organizer status does not bypass moderation.

## Community governance

Official rules, community standards, sanctions, appeals, and moderator roles must be published. Major River Expeditions should involve cultural and environmental review. Sponsored missions must label commercial involvement and cannot exchange athlete data for participation.

## Metrics

Measure meaningful participation: athletes contributing, completion, return rate, encouragement given/received, crew health, club-independent participation, safety incidents, reports resolved, and athlete sentiment. Do not optimize raw posting volume. See [16_METRICS.md](16_METRICS.md).
